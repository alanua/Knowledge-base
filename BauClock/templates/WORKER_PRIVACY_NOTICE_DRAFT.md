# BauClock — Worker Privacy Notice Draft

## Status

Draft worker-facing privacy notice for BauClock onboarding.
This is a product template, not final legal advice.

---

## 1. Why you receive this notice

Your employer/company uses BauClock for working-time and site-attendance related workflows.
This notice explains what data may be processed, for what purpose, and how the system works at a practical level.

---

## 2. Main processing purposes

The system may be used for:
- check-in / check-out recording
- break recording
- assignment to company and site
- display of worked time summaries
- payment-support workflows
- dispute handling where relevant
- security and audit logging for sensitive administrative changes

---

## 3. Data categories

Depending on enabled functions, the system may process:
- your name and company assignment
- role and site assignment
- time-event data
- summary data derived from time events
- payment-support related data
- limited location/site presence data where site validation is enabled
- logs of administrative actions affecting your records

---

## 4. Location / site presence data

If GPS or site presence validation is used, it is used for site attendance verification and not for unnecessary continuous surveillance.
The company should provide a separate site/GPS notice where this applies.

---

## 5. Who can see data

Access is role-based and should be limited to legitimate company functions such as owner, site manager, accountant, and authorized support/admin roles where applicable.
You should not assume that all company users see the same data.

---

## 6. Core rule

BauClock is used as part of the employer/company operational time-tracking workflow.
This notice is meant to inform you clearly about the processing and system behavior.

---

## 7. Contact and rights

Your employer/company remains the primary contact for workforce-data processing in the employment context.
Additional privacy and contact details should be inserted here for actual use.

---

For product use, the worker should acknowledge that this notice was shown and received.
