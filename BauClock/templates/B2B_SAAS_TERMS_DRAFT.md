# BauClock — B2B SaaS Terms Draft

## Status

Draft template for client-company legal onboarding.
This is a product template, not final legal advice.

---

## 1. Parties

This agreement is entered into between:
- Provider: [BauClock legal entity / operator]
- Customer: [Client company legal name]

---

## 2. Service

BauClock provides a B2B SaaS platform for:
- working time tracking
- site attendance tracking
- role-based access
- overtime and payment-support workflows
- subcontractor / Gewerbe coordination
- accounting/export support

BauClock does not replace:
- payroll provider
- tax advisor
- accountant
- legal advisor

---

## 3. Contract scope

Customer receives a non-exclusive, non-transferable right to use the service during the subscription term within agreed plan limits.

Customer is responsible for:
- lawful internal use
- user management within company scope
- correctness of source business data
- review of payroll/accounting/legal outputs before relying on them externally

---

## 4. Roles and responsibility

Default operating model:
- Customer = controller
- BauClock provider = processor for personal data processing covered by the service

Details of processing are governed by the separate AVV / DPA.

---

## 5. Service boundaries

BauClock supports:
- technical recording
- aggregation
- workflow support
- export support

BauClock does not guarantee that any generated payroll/accounting/export output is automatically the final legally correct outcome without customer review or professional validation.

---

## 6. Customer obligations

Customer shall:
- provide accurate company and user data
- inform and onboard its workforce lawfully
- use the service in accordance with labor, privacy and workplace rules
- ensure only authorized persons access the service
- review exports and payment-related outputs before external use

---

## 7. Availability and support

Provider supplies the service according to agreed support and availability terms.
Planned maintenance, incident handling and support channels should be defined in an annex or service description.

---

## 8. Security

Provider maintains appropriate technical and organizational measures.
Customer acknowledges that security also depends on correct customer-side access management and user handling.

---

## 9. Fees

Fees may include:
- onboarding fee
- recurring subscription
- per active worker fee
- add-on module fees

Commercial details belong in order form or pricing annex.

---

## 10. Term and termination

The agreement begins on [date] and continues according to the ordered plan term unless terminated under agreed notice periods.

Upon termination, post-termination access, export and deletion/return logic follow the retention/deletion policy and DPA rules.

---

## 11. Liability

Provider liability should be limited to the extent legally permitted, subject to carve-outs required by law.
Detailed liability clause should be finalized by legal review.

---

## 12. Data processing documents

The following documents form part of the onboarding/legal pack:
- AVV / DPA
- TOMs
- privacy notice(s)
- retention/deletion policy
- subprocessor list / notice
- service description

---

## 13. Finalization note

This draft must be reviewed and finalized for real client use.
